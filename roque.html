Mask of Agamemnon

History:

The Mask of Agamemnon is a famous ancient Greek artifact discovered by archaeologist Heinrich Schliemann in 1876 at the site of Mycenae in Greece. It's a gold funeral mask, probably placed over the face of a high-ranking person during burial. While it's named after the legendary king Agamemnon, there's no solid evidence connecting it to him. The mask is known for its intricate design and is displayed in the National Archaeological Museum in Athens.
